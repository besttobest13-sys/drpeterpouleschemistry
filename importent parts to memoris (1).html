Solubility Rules

All acids are soluble in water with no exception

Cl⁻ → except AgCl, PbCl₂

Br⁻ → except AgBr, PbBr₂

I⁻ → except AgI, PbI₂

SO₄²⁻ → except PbSO₄, BaSO₄, CaSO₄

All nitrates (NO₃⁻), ammonium salts (NH₄⁺), and Group 1 salts (Na⁺, K⁺) are soluble

All bases are insoluble except:

All Group 1 hydroxides, carbonates, and oxides

Ammonium (NH₄⁺) hydroxides, carbonates, and oxides

Ba(OH)₂ and Ca(OH)₂ are slightly soluble (remember “BaCa”)

Let's Summarize: Acids, Bases, and Salts

Acid

Made of H and a non-metal.

Example: HCl, H₂SO₄

Base

Made of a metal or NH₄⁺ with OH⁻ or O²⁻.

Example: NaOH, CaO, NH₄OH

Salt

Formed when H in an acid is replaced by a metal or NH₄⁺.

Example: NaCl, NH₄Cl, MgBr₂
